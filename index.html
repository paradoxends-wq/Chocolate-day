Lindt.

Because sweetness was never your thing.
Dark chocolate doesn’t try to please.
It doesn’t soften itself to be chosen.

It expects a certain taste.
And it knows exactly who has it.
You always preferred intensity over sugar.
Cake only if it was dark.
Pastries only if they held depth.

There’s something unmistakable
about people who choose like that.
This isn’t sweetness trying to impress you.

This is desire that stays composed.
Slow.
Certain.
Fully aware of what it wants.
I know what this means to you.
And I know you deserve beautiful things.

Consider this a small token of attention —
given with intention.
— N
